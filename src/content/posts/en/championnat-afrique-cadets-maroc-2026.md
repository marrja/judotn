The delegation of the Tunisian national cadet judo team travels to the Kingdom of
Morocco to take part in the **African Cadet Judo Championship**, held on **23 and 24
July 2026**.

The entry reflects the Federation's commitment to fielding national teams across the
full range of continental and international events, and to giving our athletes the best
possible preparation — sharpening their talent, broadening their experience and
strengthening their readiness for the challenges ahead.

## National squad — girls

- Yosra Bennour (-40 kg)
- Assil Bennour (-44 kg)
- Maram Tayebi (-48 kg)
- Imen Fendri (-52 kg)
- Nour El Houda Ghribi (-57 kg)
- Ranim Ben Slimane (-63 kg)
- Khadija Trabelsi (-70 kg)
- Aya Chaib (+70 kg)

## National squad — boys

- Rayen Rgaieg (-60 kg)
- Youssef Saidi (-60 kg)

- Mohamed Ilyes Arfaoui (-73 kg)
- Ayoub Arfaoui (-81 kg)
- Iskander Aribi (-81 kg)
- Ali Kasouri (-90 kg)
- Mohamed Mallat (-90 kg)
- Iyed Aissaoui (+90 kg)

## Official delegation

- **Head of delegation:** Mr Yassine Bali, member of the federal board.
- **Technical staff:** Mr Bechir Khiari and Mr Aziz Ghezouani, national coaches.

In wishing every success to the members of our national delegation, the Tunisian Judo
Federation expresses its full confidence in the ability of our champions to compete at
their highest level, to honour the national flag, and to further secure the leading
position Tunisian judo holds across Africa.

> Every wish for success to the Eagles of Tunisia… Our flag is in your hands, and our
> confidence in your resolve knows no limit. Good luck, and every success.
